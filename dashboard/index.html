Viva Defense Quick-Reference

Expert Response Hub

Quick answers to common technical questions for your presentation.

What is Stemming?

Removing suffixes (like 'ing', 'ed') to find the "root" of the word. This simplifies the data so the AI treats 'running' and 'run' as the same concept.

Why GloVe Vectors?

These turn words into math vectors. It allows the AI to understand that 'sad' and 'unhappy' are similar in meaning, even if they are spelled differently.

What is K-Means?

An "unsupervised" tool that finds themes in the 1.6M posts without us telling it what to look for. It grouped your data into 'Work', 'Emotions', and 'Life' automatically.

Computational Strategy

Algorithm Selection Ledger: The "Why"

Theoretical justification for the implemented stack over standard alternatives.

The Applied Stack (Implemented)

• Bi-LSTM (State-of-the-Art): Selected for its unique Temporal Backpropagation. Unlike RNNs, LSTMs solve the vanishing gradient problem, allowing the model to "remember" sentiment cues from the start of a 50-word tweet and link them to the end.
• Conv1D-CNN (Efficiency): Used as a parallel "Pattern Matcher." CNNs are elite at extracting local spatial relationships (e.g., specific 3-word emotional triggers) while remaining 4x faster than LSTMs during training.
• Multinomial Naive Bayes (Baseline): Implemented to provide a "Statistical Floor." It serves as the baseline to prove that Deep Learning is actually worth the computational cost here.

Dismissed Models (Theoretical Exclusion)

• SVM / Kernel Trick: Dismissed due to Quadratic Scaling (O(n²)). On 1.6M records, SVM training time would exceed 100+ hours. It also struggles with the non-linear high-dimensional linguistic sparsity present in Twitter data.
• Random Forest / XGBoost: Excluded because they lack inherent "Sentence Order" awareness. They treat tweets as unordered "Bags of Words," losing the specific word-order context required for detecting sarcasm.
• Transformer / BERT: While high accuracy, they were considered "Overkill" for this mobile-friendly forensics demo due to extreme VRAM requirements during real-time inference.

Why is Bi-LSTM the best for this project?

In the context of Data Mining for social forensics, word order is everything. Traditional models like Naive Bayes treat sentences as "Bags of Words," losing the nuance of sarcasm. Bi-LSTM wins because its "Deep Memory" can link words from the beginning of a tweet to those at the end, effectively solving the Vanishing Gradient Problem and capturing the full emotional trajectory of the 1.6M records.
