# Research Report: Data Integrity & Reliability Audit

This document establishes the epistemic foundation of the Sentiment Analysis project, addressing the sources, biases, and engineering assumptions behind the dataset.

## 1. Dataset Provenance
- **Source**: Sentiment140 (Generated by researchers at Stanford University).
- **Scale**: 1,600,000 tweets (1.59M post-cleaning).
- **Format**: Polarity (0: Negative, 4: Positive), ID, Date, User, Text.

## 2. Sampling Bias & Population
- **Audited Risk**: High. The dataset consists of tweets from 2009. The "Active User" bias is significant; results represent the sentiment of a specific demographic (early Twitter adopters) and may not generalize to 2024 internet slang.
- **Contextual Bias**: The training set is balanced (50/50), which may not represent real-world "neutral" Twitter streams.

## 3. Linguistic Integrity (The Twitter Anomaly)
- **Statistical Fact**: Twitter language does NOT follow standard English distribution.
- **Evidence**: Chi-Square test (p-value: 0.0) conducted on this dataset confirms a significant deviation from formal linguistic expected frequencies.
- **Noise Type**: Typos, abbreviations (u, 2, r), and excessive punctuation are treated as features, not just noise.

## 4. Preprocessing Assumptions
- **Handle Removal**: Assumed that `@user` tokens carry zero sentiment value and are noise.
- **Stemming**: Used Snowball Stemming to reduce dimensionality. Assumption: "Running" and "Run" carry identical sentiment weight.
- **Empty Rows**: All tweets reduced to 0 length after cleaning were purged (Total purged: ~7,672).

## 5. Known Limitations
- **Sarcasm Blind Spot**: The models (LSTM/CNN) primarily rely on word frequency and local context. Sarcasm, which requires world-knowledge, remains a primary source of error (~21% total inaccuracy).
- **Emoji Handling**: This specific version of the dataset (Sentiment140 "no emoticons") stripped emojis, losing the single most potent source of sentiment signal on Twitter.
